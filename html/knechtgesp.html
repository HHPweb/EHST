Dr. Paul Knechtges

Hometown: Cofield, North Carolina

Education:
B.S. (summa cum laude) in Environmental Health, East Carolina University, 1981
M.S. in Environmental Science, George Washington University, 1988
Ph.D. in Environmental Biology & Public Policy, George Mason University, 1995

What inspired him to choose Environmental Health: He was initially inspired by the environmental movement of the 1960s and 1970s. In addition, he spent his teenage years living along the banks and woods of the Chowan River in Hertford County, where he developed a great appreciation for nature. Eventually, job opportunities led him to specialize in environmental health.

Why he chose to teach at East Carolina: After a long military career, he wanted to return home and teach at his alma mater.

How long he has taught Environmental Health at ECU: Part-time since fall of 2006.

What classes he teaches: He currently teaches the food safety course and principles of toxicology course. He has also taught courses in the fundamentals of environmental health, biological safety, and system safety.

What he most enjoys about teaching: He enjoys sharing his knowledge and practical experiences with the students, as well as keeping abreast of the latest advances in the science and practice of environmental health. Also, he enjoys the collaborations and interactions with faculty and students since it provides intellectual stimulation.

His concentration within the Environmental Health field, as well as other involvement with the field: His current concentration is safety of the food supply, especially since he has written a textbook on that subject. In addition, based on his past job experience, he has expertise and interest in toxicology, which has relevance in all environmental health fields.
