Mr. Joseph Sutton

Hometown: Greenville, NC

Education:
B.S. in Health Education: Worksite Health Promotion, East Carolina University, 2005
M.S. in Environmental Health, East Carolina University, 2008

What inspired him to choose Environmental Health: He has always been interested in exploring the outdoors and he grew up on a farm for the majority of his life. He is interested in nature and has often been curious as to how humans affect nature by our actions and activities.

Why he chose to teach at East Carolina: East Carolina is his alma mater and teaching creates a way for him to give back to the university.

How long he has taught Environmental Health at ECU: Since Fall 2013.

What classes he teaches: Introduction to Environmental Health.

What he most enjoys about teaching: He enjoys being able to relate real world experience in a classroom setting.

His concentration within the Environmental Health field, as well as other involvement with the field: His concentration was the study of vectors under the late Dr. Alice Anderson. His research was related to the biodiversity of mosquitoes in urban and rural populations. Previously, he held a position at UNC Chapel Hill conducting OSHA inspections, quantitative fit testing, accident investigations, policy development, and trend analysis of accidents. More recently, he held a position at East Carolina University as the Hazardous Waste Manager, which consisted of managing the hazardous and universal waste, testing fume hoods, and calibrating industrial hygiene equipment.
