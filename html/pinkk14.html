Our MSEH Alumni Success

Kathryn Pink '14

Career: Environmental Health Specialist at Durham County Department of Public Health

What are your responsibilities in your current EH job?

  • Inspect food service establishments, lodging and institutions as well as childcare facilities and schools
  • Investigate complaints and foodborne illness
  • Follow up on required verifications for critical violations
  • Assist with applying for variances and creating HACCP plans
  • Translate for Spanish-speakers when needed

What are some of your accomplishments as an EH professional?

  • I gained authorization to inspect food, lodging and institutions within 4 months of employment.
  • I gained authorization to inspect childcare facilities and schools within 7 months of employment.
  • I passed the state Registered Environmental Health Specialist (REHS) Exam within 9 months of employment.
  • I was elected as the secretary/treasurer of the North Central Environmental Health District (represents 12 counties)
  • I was designated the position of Intern Coordinator for a NEHA-sponsored internship site.

What do you like most about your career?
My day is never the same. I schedule what locations I would like to visit each day and there's always something unique, new or interesting (or all three) about each inspection! There's lots of opportunity for training and building skills, both interpersonal and technical.

How do you think your ECU EH education prepared you for your career?
When I started working at Durham County I was already familiar with some items like HACCP, food safety and regulations from courses in the MSEH program, so I had a good base to work off of. I think choosing a topic for my professional paper that required me to speak with and interview strangers about health and safety was helpful as well since that is what I do every day now. My ECU EH education gave me a leg-up in my career as an EH Specialist because with an EH degree, your state internship requirement is only one year instead of two (all other degrees with 30 semester hours of science are required to do two years) and it prepared me to take the state board [REHS] exam which covers topics like waste water management which aren't part of my scope on a daily basis but are part of the scope of a registered environmental health specialist. I passed on my first attempt and there's a 50% failure rate! It's hard!
