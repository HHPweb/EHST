Our BSEH Alumni Success

Chelsea Curry '13

Career: Environmental, Health, and Safety Coordinator at Tyson Foods, Inc.

What are your responsibilities in your current EH job?
My main job function is to manage the safety program elements to maintain compliance with NCDOL [NC Department of Labor], DOT [Department of Transportation], and NCDEQ [NC Department of Environmental Quality] requirements for a World Class Commercial Bakery. I've had the opportunity to work with regulatory agencies during audit processes. In addition, I have had opportunity to learn how different departments have to work together to ensure the safety and health of the environment, people, and property. Although, I would have to say that the best responsibility that I have is to attend various trainings in order to earn certifications related to the EH&S field.

What do you like most about your career?
I like how this career field is challenging, rewarding, and every day is completely different from the one before.

How do you think your ECU EH education prepared you for your career?
I believe my ECU EH education prepared me exceptionally well--so well, in fact, that I was honestly prepared for many more jobs than "just" an Environmental Health specialist. This is noted by the fact that, although my title includes Environmental Health, I was hired for a role that is more safety oriented than anything else. In addition, I could have easily have applied for jobs related to industrial sanitation or food safety and quality assurance and felt very confident during the interview process because of the knowledge I gained in those areas during my time as an EH undergraduate.
