Dr. Charles Humphrey

Hometown: Jasper, NC

Education:
BS from North Carolina State University 1996 (Ecosystems Assessment)
MS from North Carolina State University 2002 (Soil Science)
PhD from East Carolina University 2010 (Coastal Resources Management: Geosciences Concentration)

What inspired him to choose Environmental Health: Having grown up in a rural town, he always had an interest and appreciation for natural resources and the environment.

Why he chose to teach at East Carolina: The Environmental Health Sciences Program at ECU has a long history of serving NC and is recognized by EH professionals for the excellent preparation that the faculty provides their students.

How long he has taught Environmental Health at ECU: Since 2009.

What classes he teaches: Safe Water; Wastewater Management; Introduction to Environmental Health; Technical Advances in Water Supply and Wastewater Treatment; and Professional Paper/Thesis Writing Series.

What he most enjoys about teaching: Meeting new students and hopefully inspiring them to make a positive influence on their communities and the environment.

His concentration within the Environmental Health field, as well as other involvement with the field: His research and teaching focus mostly on water quality and wastewater treatment.
