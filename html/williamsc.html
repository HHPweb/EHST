Our BSEH Alumni Success

Chelsea Williams '14

Career: Industrial Hygienist with the Directorate for Public Health, Naval Hospital Camp Lejeune

Unit Mission: The mission for Industrial Hygiene, Directorate for Public Health is to anticipate, recognize, evaluate and control environmental factors which cause sickness, impair health and well-being for Active Duty, Civilian, and Contractors onboard Camp Lejeune and its surrounding Department of Defense (DoD) installations.

What are your responsibilities in your current EH job?
Responsibilities of this job include planning and conducting complete evaluations of work operations on Camp Lejeune's base; to assess worker exposures to chemical, physical, and biological hazards, analyze findings, and recommend corrective controls to reduce or eliminate the exposure of the hazardous condition. We perform different types of sampling such as noise dosimetry, air sampling, ventilation readings, respiratory fit testing, indoor air quality issues, etc.

What do you like most about your job?
The people in the Directorate for Public Health that I work in are very friendly, encouraging and helpful to help one another to get jobs completed, that makes coming to work every day a lot better! I really enjoy working on a military base and able to incorporate industrial hygiene methods into the military work ethics. One day I may be doing an onsite walkthrough survey at the air station with the ospreys and helicopters, the next day doing indoor air quality sampling at the hospital, and ending the week with lead wipe sampling at the armory. Every day is a different and exciting day at the office, and you never know what to expect!

How do you think your ECU EH education prepared you for your career?
The first day of my Introduction to Environmental Health Sciences course, I knew I wanted to pursue a career with this degree. This degree allows you to never have a normal workday at the office behind the computer. My professors in the environmental health program and internship at the ECU Environmental Health and Safety Office allowed me to enhance my ability to work on multiple projects at one time, and to always make a deadline for a report or project. My industrial hygiene and laboratory courses allowed me to have experience in working with the equipment and monitoring devices that we work with on a day to day basis at the office.
