Our BSEH Student Internship Experiences

Here are words from our alumni and students regarding their internship experiences:

William "Cody" Goodrich '16
  • Workplace Hygiene, Greensboro, NC

"I completed my internship with Workplace Hygiene, which is an industrial hygiene consulting firm for many different industries... My job title was Junior Industrial Hygienist. I assisted Certified Industrial Hygienists with calibration of equipment, sampling, and report preparation. The types of sampling we conducted were air sampling, noise monitoring, respirator fit testing, pulmonary function testing, indoor air quality surveys, and heat stress monitoring."

Loc Nguyen '16
  • Department of Human and Health Services (DHHS) of North Carolina, Raleigh, NC

"My internship was at the Department of Human and Health Services (DHHS) of North Carolina in Raleigh, NC. I was referred to this particular location by NEHA [National Environmental Health Association] through their National Environmental Public Health Internship in 2015. At DHHS, I was able to learn new skills and gained more knowledge about environmental health, especially the food and lodging services, as well as the public health branch of the department, particularly in the Epidemiology department... I was able to experience how an Environmental Health professional spend their day. There are so many aspects in the EH field that I never knew about, and the internship absolutely helped me out with that."

Kayla Pethel '16
  • Bunbury Wildlife Park, a non-profit wildlife conservation park, in Bunbury, Australia

"My role during this internship was assisting the on-duty wildlife officer in all aspects. My responsibilities were to perform daily routes of feeding all animals throughout the wildlife park, as well as maintaining the exhibits and enclosures... I was blessed to have my internship in Australia, which is unlike most internships, so I was able to experience and learn from a different culture, while becoming more educated on environmental health. My favorite part of the experience was working hands-on with wildlife (i.e. exotic animals), while incorporating and performing environmental sustainability and health tasks... I was able to apply knowledge from previous classes in order to relate my daily activities to environmental health and science topics."

Melinda Fields '15
  • Pitt County Health Department, Greenville, NC

Melinda completed her internship with the Pitt County Vector Control Program. She performed various tasks with EH professionals, which included assisting in the inspection and surveying of properties, lands and facilities for presence of vectors (e.g., mosquitoes, ticks); improving public awareness and knowledge on the control and prevention of human-to-mosquito transmitted diseases; monitoring progress of vector control efforts; conducting source reduction and suggesting pest prevention measures; and responding to citizens' complaints and requests for vector control services. She was featured on WITN discussing chikungunya.

Samuel Rivera '15
  • U.S. Coast Guard, Portsmouth, VA

Samuel's responsibilities as an intern with the U.S. Coast Guard involved the conduct of quantitative respirator fit testing, noise exposure assessment by noise dosimetry, heat stress monitoring, ventilation testing, safety inspection, and air sampling for hexavalent chromium at paint shops.

Derek Cooper '14
  • U.S. Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune, Jacksonville, NC

"I performed my internship at Camp Lejeune's Department of Industrial Hygiene. My internship dealt with monitoring ventilation at different locations on the base to ensure it is working according to proper standards. Completed walk through surveys with different industrial hygienists at many of the Marine Corps maintenance buildings on Camp Lejeune. The majority of my internship involved taking numerous noise samples and doing air monitoring on the Marine Corps base Camp Lejeune, and also Marine Corps Air Station New River."
