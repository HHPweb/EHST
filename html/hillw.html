Mr. William Hill

Hometown: Kinston, North Carolina

Education:
BS in Environmental Health &Natural Resources, Delaware State University, 1983
MSA in Health Services Administration, Central Michigan University, 2003

What inspired him to choose Environmental Health: While he was stationed at Plattsburgh Air Force Base, Plattsburgh, NY, he had a conversation with a Lt. Colonel in charge of Preventative Medicine and Environmental Science.

Why he chose to teach at East Carolina: He chose to teach after a conversation with Dr. Dan Sprau at an Environmental Health meeting in Raleigh, NC, while he was working as an Environmental Health Supervisor at Craven County Health Department.

How long he has taught Environmental Health at ECU: Since 2004.

What classes he teaches: Introduction to Environmental Health Science; Principles of Food Sanitation; Institutional & Recreational Sanitation; Environmental Health Training in Emergency Response; and Solid and Hazardous Waste Technology.

What he most enjoys about teaching: He enjoys sharing his 20 years of experience in the field of Environmental Health, such as serving on rules and policy making committees with regards to consultation, interpretation, enforcement, education, and surveillance of environmental factors that could affect the public health. He also enjoys bringing "real world" experience in the areas of Food &Lodging; Tattoo &Body Piercing; Onsite Wastewater; Private Water Supplies; Institutional Sanitation; Food Safety; Childcare Facilities; Insect, Vector and Rodent Control; Solid Waste Disposal; Community Surveys; School Sanitation; Local Confinement Facilities; Adult Day Care Facilities; and Public Swimming Pools and School Buildings.

His concentration within the Environmental Health field, as well as other involvement with the field: His concentration is in the area of Food Safety. He was previously employed as an Environmental Health Supervisor, Environmental Program Specialist, and Environmental Health Specialist in the Food &Lodging section of Environmental Health. His responsibilities included: education and enforcement of rules and regulations as they pertain to all foodservice establishments; teaching food safety classes for school cafeteria workers, child care employees, and restaurant employees; and using food safety principles in the inspection of hospitals, nursing homes, hotels, bed &breakfast inns, concessions stands at sporting events, mass gatherings, community food festivals, meat markets, summer camps, and local confinement facilities.
