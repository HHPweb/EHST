MSEH Student Activities

The graduate and undergraduate students of the East Carolina University (ECU) Environmental Health Sciences Program assist in conducting a mosquito field research project led by Dr. Stephanie Richards as the principal investigator. The research team sets carbon dioxide-baited CDC light traps to collect host-seeking mosquitoes, and ovitraps to monitor container-ovipositing mosquitoes like Aedes albopictus.

The graduate and undergraduate students of the ECU Environmental Health Sciences Program describe their research projects as they present their posters during the Research and Creative Achievement Week (RCAW) 2016 at East Carolina University.

Heat stress and ultraviolet radiation monitoring to assess exposure of ECU groundskeepers at Greenville, NC

Installation of a monitoring well in Durham, NC

Measurement of stream flow and sampling of stream water in Durham, NC

Measurement of the width of the creek in Cedar Point, NC

Collecting mosquito eggs in stagnant water
