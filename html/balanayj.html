Dr. Jo Anne Balanay

Hometown: Philippines

Education:
B.S. in Public Health, University of the Philippines Manila, 1997
M.S. in Environmental Health-Industrial Hygiene, University of Minnesota, 2003
Master of Occupational Health, University of the Philippines Manila, 2005
Ph.D. in Environmental Health-Industrial Hygiene, University of Alabama at Birmingham, 2011

What inspired her to choose Environmental Health: She hopes to make a difference in protecting the working population, especially the understudied ones.

Why she chose to teach at East Carolina: She believes that teaching environmental and occupational health to undergraduate students, not just graduate students, will better instill in young minds about the relationship between the environment and human health, and about how students can make a difference if they choose to be Environmental Health professionals.

How long she has taught Environmental Health at ECU: Since Fall 2011.

What classes she teaches: Introduction to Occupational Health; Environmental Issues in Construction; Industrial Hygiene (undergraduate level); and Industrial Hygiene Applications (graduate level).

What she most enjoys about teaching: She most enjoys sharing with students what she does best: Industrial Hygiene.

Her concentration within the Environmental Health field, as well as other involvement with the field: Her concentrations are Occupational Health and Industrial Hygiene. She is also involved in research on the application of activated carbon fibers for respiratory protection and research on occupational exposure assessment among outdoor workers such as farmers and foresters.
