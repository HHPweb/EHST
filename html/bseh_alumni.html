What advice do you have for students considering EH as a degree?
"Do it. I can't say anything negative any way. I made the decision to major in environmental health and I have never regretted it. The job placement rates are off the charts. I'm not just talking about jobs in the public sector but jobs in the private sector too."

Corey Woolard '02

Career: Director of Operations for Esschem Polymers

How did your EH career lead you to your current position?
After spending nearly a decade in the EHS field and obtaining my CIH [Certified Industrial Hygienists] and CSP [Certified Safety Professional] certifications, I transitioned my career to Operations Management. Starting my career in EHS within the chemical industry enabled me to gain fundamental knowledge of chemical processes, exposure routes and assessments, and understand the key dynamics of educating employees regarding EHS and industrial hygiene topics. This was an inherent foundation that prepared me for career advancement, as I used the technical acumen from industrial hygiene, safety, and chemistry; and applied it to continuously improve and develop my professional life. From there, I was able to build a body of work that transferred into operations, where I'm still able to utilize my technical skill sets and add business value to the organization in which I'm employed.

How do you think your ECU EH education prepared you for your career?
It was my fundamental starting point... it gave me the technical knowledge I needed to begin my career journey and build upon it!
